<p><p>The flame retardant fiber market is divided into two primary types: staple fiber and long fiber. Staple fibers are short, discrete lengths that are typically used in textiles, upholstery, and protective clothing, providing flexibility and ease of processing. In contrast, long fibers, also known as filament fibers, are continuous strands that offer enhanced durability and strength. They are commonly used in applications such as industrial fabrics and automotive interiors, where superior performance and fire resistance are crucial.</p></p>
