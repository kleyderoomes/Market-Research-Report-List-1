<p><p>The femoral head prostheses market is primarily categorized into two types: ceramic and metal prostheses. Ceramic prostheses are known for their biocompatibility and reduced wear rates, making them ideal for younger, active patients. They offer smooth articulation but can be brittle under certain conditions. Metal prostheses, typically made from titanium or cobalt-chromium alloys, are more durable and can withstand higher loads, making them suitable for a broader patient demographic. Both types aim to restore mobility and enhance quality of life after hip joint deterioration.</p></p>
