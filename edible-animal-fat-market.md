<p><p>The edible animal fat market can be categorized into three main types: liquid, solid, and semi-solid. Liquid fats, such as certain oils and melted fats, are often used for frying and cooking due to their ease of use. Solid fats, like lard and tallow, provide structure in baking and are ideal for recipes requiring firm textures. Semi-solid fats, including margarine and shortening, offer versatility, blending properties of both liquid and solid fats, and are commonly used in both culinary and processed food applications.</p></p>
