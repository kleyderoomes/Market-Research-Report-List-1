<p><ul><li>Vacuum Melting Method</li><li>Carrier Gas Melting Method</li><li>Azeotropic Vapor Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Polyester polyols for rigid foam are produced through various methods which influence their properties and applications. The vacuum melting method involves melting the polyol under reduced pressure, minimizing moisture and impurities. The carrier gas melting method utilizes an inert gas to improve heat transfer and product quality. The azeotropic vapor method employs solvents to create a vapor that helps in the polymerization process, enhancing the final foam characteristics. Each method yields polyols suited for different rigid foam applications in industries like construction and automotive.</p></p>
